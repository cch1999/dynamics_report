Symbolic distance matrices with KeOps


It is very common in sturctural bioinformaics

In stead of storing a large matrix populated with precomuted values, symbolic matrices simply store the data required to generate the matrix (e.g. two vectors/matrices) and the equations needed to compute the elements in the matrix. In the case of protein distance matrices, the only data stored are the Cartesian coordinates of every atom (x and y, where x is the transpose of y) and the Pythagorean theorem. This allows for substantial reductions in memory costs, as we can perform efficient reductions over the symbolic distance matrices. In our case, we can effectively compute which are the K-nearest neighbours without needed to calculate a whole dense matrix.

Whether you are assuming the protein graph is fully connected (like in Greener & Jones (2021) Greener, J. G. & Jones, D. T. (2021),
‘Differentiable molecular simulation can learn all the parameters in a coarse-grained force field for proteins’,
bioRxiv
doi:10.1101/2021.02.05.429941) or only locally connected (like in ours), some form of distance matrix needs to be calculated at every step in the DMS.
